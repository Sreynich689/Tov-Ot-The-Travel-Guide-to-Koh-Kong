Koh Kong
Overview

Koh Kong stands out as an ideal destination for travelers yearning for authentic nature, adventure, and tranquility, a welcome escape from the throngs of Cambodia’s more frequented islands.

This province is a treasure trove of natural wonders, offering experiences that connect you deeply with its pristine environment. Don't miss these must-see attractions within Koh Kong province.

Geography

Koh Kong is one of the southwest provinces of Cambodia which is 290 kilometers from Phnom Penh along National Road 4 and 48. Koh Kong, covering 10,045.58 square kilometers, is one of the coastal provinces of Cambodia.

Koh Kong is bordered by provinces of Pursat to the north, Kampong Speu to the east, Preah Sihanouk and Kampot to the southwest. Its coastal position provides access to the sea, making it an important area for marine and coastal activities

History

Koh Kong blends rich history with diverse landscapes.

Early history

Koh Kong has been inhabited for centuries and served as a coastal trade hub linked to the Khmer Empire and neighboring cultures.

Ancient jar burials

In Choam Sla village (Chi Phat commune), 15th–17th century jar burials and log coffins reveal a unique, possibly royal burial tradition of the Chung people a rare and mysterious archaeological site.

Colonial era

In the 19th–20th centuries Koh Kong became contested between France and Siam. Ceded to France in 1904, it joined French Indochina. Coastal settlements were small; a postal steamship linked the area to Saigon and Bangkok.Under King Sisowath Monivong (1927–1941), Koh Kong was a district of Kampot province.
